## 1. Research findings

### 1.1 ED ambulance diversion (item 5)

**What it is.** When an ED can't safely accept more ambulance patients,
the charge nurse + ED physician initiate a temporary status that EMS
honors by routing ambulances elsewhere. Two tiers:

- **Critical-care bypass** — the ED cannot accept ONE more critically
  ill patient. Effectively closed to ambulances.
- **Redirect consideration** — the ED is stretched. Non-critical
  ambulance patients should be routed elsewhere; criticals still come.

**Decision criteria.** Must have first exhausted internal resources:
call-back staff, expedite discharges, open hallway beds, page house
sup. Diversion is the *last resort*, not the first move.

**Authority.** Charge nurse + ED physician (and ED manager when
available) make the call together. House supervisor / Admin On-Call
notified.

**Duration.** Auto-terminate is typical: 2h for full divert, 4h for
redirect. Hospital must re-affirm if extending.

**Notifications.** EMS regional dispatch, neighboring EDs, hospital
operator, house sup, admin on call, regional medical control.

**EMTALA constraint.** Diversion does NOT relieve EMTALA obligations
if a patient still presents at the door.

Sources:
- [Annals · Guidelines for ambulance diversion](https://www.annemergmed.com/article/S0196-0644(00)29318-9/abstract)
- [Pitt · Hospital Ambulance-Diversion Policies](https://sites.pitt.edu/~kconover/ftp/diversion.pdf)
- [StatPearls · EMS ED Diversion](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK603736/)
- [Holland & Hart · Sample EMTALA Diversion Policy](https://www.hhhealthlawblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/EMTALA-Diversion-Policy-Sample.pdf)

**PULSE divert flow design.**

A 5-step overlay sheet anchored to the charge nurse's existing context:

1. **Check internal resources** (checklist auto-populated from PULSE
   data): hallway beds open · discharges in flight · float pool
   available · house sup paged.
2. **Pick tier** — Critical Care Bypass · Redirect · Cancel.
3. **Set duration** — pre-set 2h or 4h, custom up to 8h.
4. **Notify** — auto-checked list: EMS dispatch · neighbor EDs · house
   sup · admin on call. Each row shows status (pending / sent / ack).
5. **Confirm** — big rose CTA. Status posts to HUD ticker + bed-board
   header. Auto-reminder fires 30m before expiration.

After confirm: an "ON DIVERT · 1h 42m left" banner sits across the
top HUD until cleared or expired. Tapping it re-opens the sheet with
a "Stand down" button.

### 1.2 Proactive AI in clinical ops (item 14)

**Reactive AI** waits for a user query. **Proactive (agentic) AI**:

- monitors continuously
- anticipates needs from patterns + thresholds
- takes action OR pre-stages action with a HITL gate
- shows what it did and why

State of the art in 2025/2026:

- 80%+ of health systems prioritize agentic AI for ops + revenue cycle
- Patient flow, bed mgmt, surge prediction, scheduling are the most
  common targets
- Production HITL workflows see human-intervention rates drop from
  35-45% in month 1 to 8-15% by month 4 as the agent learns
- Anticipatory risk stratification > reactive monitoring

Sources:
- [Becker's · Autonomous & proactive guide](https://go.beckershospitalreview.com/hrwp/autonomous-proactive-a-healthcare-guide-for-ais-3rd-era)
- [Deloitte · Agentic AI in healthcare](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/health-care/agentic-ai-health-care-operating-model-change.html)
- [ARC · Clinical Decision Support to Autonomous Care](https://www.arcweb.com/blog/clinical-decision-support-autonomous-care-emerging-role-ai-healthcare-operations)
- [Wipro · Anticipate, Assist, Resolve](https://www.wipro.com/business-process/articles/anticipate-assist-resolve-agentic-ai-and-proactive-member-engagement-in-healthcare/)
- [Plivo · HITL Patterns in production](https://www.plivo.com/blog/human-in-the-loop-patterns-for-ai-customer-service-in-production/)

**PULSE proactive AI design.**

PULSE.AI is a continuously-running agent layer that:

- **Watches** every signal stream (alerts, labs, vitals, capacity,
  inbound EMS, staff fatigue, calls)
- **Acts autonomously** on mechanical / well-bounded tasks (paging,
  scheduling lab redraws, dispatching EVS, posting status updates)
- **Escalates to human** on clinical-judgment items via the HITL gate
  (already implemented in the call feature; same pattern global)
- **Surfaces a feed** on every screen where it acted, plus a dedicated
  PULSE.AI activity surface

Visual implementation:

- Inline **AI badge** on any item the AI handled or proposed (rose
  glyph + tooltip explaining what + why + confidence)
- **AI Activity strip** across the top of Horizon, showing the last
  N actions taken (auto-executed) + pending HITL items
- **Manual / Auto** column on every Action / Task row, mirroring the
  call-feature pattern
- **Confidence chips** — HIGH (no chip), MED (small dot), LOW (rose
  "Review" chip)

### 1.3 Hospital staffing AI (item 16)

**What it does.**

- Predict shift demand from historical data + seasonality + patient
  acuity
- Match open shifts to most-cost-effective option (employees → float
  pool → on-call → per diem → agency)
- Forecast call-outs, suggest swaps, identify burnout/fatigue risk
- Auto-assign within rule sets; surface conflicts for human review

Real-world results: Ascension reduced contract-labor dependency 15%
in 6 months while keeping patient-satisfaction stable.

Sources:
- [Cleveland Clinic · AI in staffing](https://consultqd.clevelandclinic.org/how-ai-assists-with-staffing-scheduling-and-once-tedious-tasks)
- [In-House Health platform](https://www.inhouse.health/)
- [symplr · Predictive Healthcare Staffing](https://www.symplr.com/blog/predictive-healthcare-staffing-ai-innovation)
- [Workeen · AI Nurse Shift Planner Guide](https://workeen.ai/healthcare-scheduling-software-ai-nurse-shift-planner/)

**PULSE Staffing redesign.**

Three regions on one full-screen surface:

1. **Now strip** — live: who's on, who's overdue for a break, who's
   doing back-to-back triple, current ratios vs target.
2. **AI Suggestions** — proposed actions the AI wants approval for:
   "Float Nurse Diaz from Med-Surg → ED (89% match, +1 RN, expires in
   2h)" with Approve/Edit/Reject buttons. Auto-executed items (e.g.,
   sent shift-swap requests) appear as DONE entries with timestamps.
3. **Schedule grid** — 12h ahead, color-coded by gap risk (green safe,
   amber risk, rose critical). Drag-to-reassign + AI-suggested fills.

Insights cards: fatigue score, target-ratio adherence over last 7
days, top swap-request reasons, projected overtime cost.

### 1.4 Jira-for-hospital · Actions (item 18)

**Jira's UX patterns:**

- **Board view** with swimlanes by status (To Do · In Progress · Done)
- **List view** for raw triage
- **Priority** (P0-P4 or fixed labels), **Assignee**, **Type**,
  **Reporter**, **Due**, **Labels**, **Linked items**
- **Filtering** — quick filters on the board, JQL for power users
- **Automation rules** — auto-assign by type, auto-escalate by SLA
- Healthcare implementations use it for patient flow, discharge
  pathways, IT service mgmt

Sources:
- [Growth Natives · JIRA in healthcare](https://growthnatives.com/blogs/jira/revolutionizing-healthcare-management-with-jira-a-project-tracking-breakthrough/)
- [Atlassian · Jira ticketing 2026](https://clearfeed.ai/blogs/jira-service-management-ticketing-system-guide)
- [Atlassian Community · Jira prioritization 2026](https://community.atlassian.com/forums/App-Central-articles/Your-Guide-to-Jira-Prioritization-2026/ba-p/3096276)

**PULSE Actions redesign.**

Single global Actions/Tasks store unified with the comms task store
(items 18 + 19 connect). Surfaces:

- **Actions tab** (full-screen): board view (default) with columns
  STAT / Routine / FYI by priority OR Proposed / Approved / Running /
  Done by status. Toggle. Quick filters: mine · all · by unit · by
  source (call/lab/EHR/AI-proactive).
- **Comms tab → Tasks tile** always visible in the right column
  (currently is, just needs the alwaysvisible enforcement).
- **Card detail** opens a side sheet: text, AI reasoning, source
  link (the call / alert / lab), assignee, history.
- **AI heavy use**: the AI is the default "reporter". Routine + FYI
  auto-execute (proven safe). STAT + low-confidence items wait in
  Proposed.

### 1.5 ED shift performance metrics (item 21)

**Five dimensions** (from systematic review):

1. **Quality of care** — mistake events, sentinel events, return
   visits within 72h
2. **Patient flow** — door-to-disposition time, length of stay,
   left-without-being-seen rate
3. **Timeliness** — door-to-doc, door-to-bed, ED boarding hours
4. **Costs** — overtime, contract labor, per-visit cost
5. **Resources** — nurse-to-patient ratio adherence, bed utilization

**Top metrics worth surfacing:**

- Door-to-disposition median (target < 4h)
- LWBS rate (target < 2%)
- RN:patient ratio adherence
- Median alarm-to-action time
- Median handoff prep duration
- Tasks resolved / shift
- Manual approvals queued (HITL gate)

Sources:
- [PMC · ED Quality Dashboard systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8366462/)
- [AHRQ · Measuring ED Performance](https://www.ahrq.gov/research/findings/final-reports/ptflow/section3.html)
- [Optum · ED Performance Metrics](https://business.optum.com/en/insights/emergency-department-performance-metrics.html)

**Brief Me redesign.**

Currently lives at `components/BriefMe.tsx` + `clinical/BriefMeScreen.tsx`.
Reshape into a **shift-performance dashboard** with three sections:

1. **End-of-shift summary** — 6 big metric cards from the list above
   with trend vs prior shift / 7-day median.
2. **What happened** — auto-narrative composed from PULSE event log
   ("4h 12m surge · 2 escalations · 14 AI-resolved tasks · 0 misses").
3. **Handoff draft** (SBAR) — auto-prepared from your shift's data,
   editable, copy / share.
