## Design Context

### Users

**Primary visitor type: cold inbound, evaluative mindset.**

- Hospital executives (CMO, COO, CNO) sent the link by a board member
- Investors lurking before a partner meeting
- Recruiters' candidates (engineers, designers) deciding whether to take the call
- Clinicians referred by a colleague

**Their state of mind on arrival:**

- Low context — they don't know what PULSE is yet
- Short patience — 60 seconds to understand "what is this?"
- High stakes — they're deciding whether PULSE is worth their next hour
- Evaluative — they're scoring credibility, not browsing for fun

**Job to be done:**

| Time | What they need |
|------|----------------|
| 0–10s | Understand the wedge: "90-minute operational picture for hospitals" |
| 10–60s | Believe the team can build this — visually and substantively |
| 1–3min | Decide whether to book a demo or send to a colleague |
| 3min+ | Know what to do next: **demo** for ops/clinicians, **investor inquiry** for investors |

**Conversion moments:**
- Primary: "Request a demo" (warm-led from any section)
- Secondary: "For investors" (separate route, lower-key, never compete with demo CTA)

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** powerful · professional · sophisticated

**Voice:**
- Technical, not cold. Speak like a senior clinician who's also a clear writer — precise but humane.
- Confident, not arrogant. State the claim, show the evidence, move on. No swagger, no hedging.
- Precise, not bureaucratic. Specific numbers, specific times, specific roles. Avoid healthcare-SaaS filler ("comprehensive", "robust", "best-in-class", "next-generation", "unified platform").

**Feeling we want a visitor to leave with:**
- "A system you'd trust at 3am."
- "These people respect my time."
- "This is the most thought-through tool I've seen in this category."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark. Confirmed against v1.

**Aesthetic register: tactical HUD, executed with much more restraint than v1.**

Same vocabulary inherited from `/Users/nickrapoport/Documents/PULSE/components/design/tokens.ts`:
- Near-black canvas `#050505`
- Geist sans + Geist Mono pair
- Rose-600 accent `#E11D48`
- Sharp 2px radii
- Corner brackets, monospace data labels, scanline ambient

**The divergence from v1 is editorial pacing, not vocabulary swap:**

| Where v1 does this... | ...v2 does this |
|---|---|
| 3 spec cards stacked next to the hero chart | 1 spec card, more space, slower reveal |
| Rose-600 used on every label, dot, accent | Rose-600 used 1× per scene — on the most important number only |
| Section eyebrow + headline + subhead + CTA all packed in viewport | Headline holds the screen alone before content arrives |
| ~67 viewports of scroll across 11 sections | Same beat count, but each scene breathes longer |
| Synthetic HUD tiles for every "video" position | Authentic product UI screenshots where possible; synthetic only when product UI doesn't exist yet |

**Reference (overall feel + motion):** [toptier.relats.com](https://toptier.relats.com/) — premium industrial product page. Single-color hero macro. Paced storytelling. Restrained palette. Editorial weight.

**Anti-references — never look like:**
- **epic.com** — bloated healthcare-SaaS, conservative corporate, dense feature lists, generic stock imagery
- **discover.oxos.com/mc2workflow** — typical healthcare-SaaS landing page template (hero + 3-column features + testimonial carousel + CTA)
- AI-startup landing pages with rainbow gradients, glassmorphic everything, "trusted by the world's best teams" logo grids, hero illustrations with floating UI windows
- Smiling-doctor stock photography (zero tolerance — never)

**Forbidden visual moves (project-specific bans, in addition to impeccable's standard absolute_bans):**
- No stock photos of clinicians, hospitals, or patients
- No left/right colored stripe accents on cards (impeccable BAN 1)
- No gradient text or `background-clip: text` (impeccable BAN 2)
- No "trusted by" logo grids
- No icon-on-rounded-square pattern above feature headings
- No glassmorphic CTA buttons — use solid surfaces only
- No floating product-UI screenshot mockups tilted at 15° with glow
- No rose-600 in more than one location per viewport at any scroll position

### Design Principles

Five principles derived from the brief — every design decision in v2 must pass these:

1. **Restraint over density.** When in doubt, remove. v1 over-packed scenes. v2 lets each beat hold the screen on its own.

2. **One accent per scene.** Rose-600 is a punctuation mark, not a paint. It lands on the single most important number or word per pinned beat, and on no other element in that beat.

3. **Editorial pacing.** Headlines hold alone. Pinned scrubs commit to longer beats. The visitor never feels rushed *or* dragged — every scene earns its scroll length.

4. **Data is voice.** Expertise is shown by how a number is presented (mono labels, confidence brackets, time-to-event indicators, driver decomposition) — not by chrome, glow, or decoration.

5. **Anti-stock authenticity.** Imagery is product UI, abstract HUD, or genuinely documentary. If we don't have something authentic to show, we use type and silence instead.
